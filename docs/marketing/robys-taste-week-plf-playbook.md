# Roby's Taste Week — 7-Day PLF Pilot

Status: campaign design only; no public offer is activated by this document.

## 1. Pilot thesis

Roby's should not launch another webpage. It should launch a new way to choose a coffee moment:

> Pick the feeling. Roby's reveals one drink-and-dessert pairing for today.

The pilot tests whether a short, story-led launch creates more qualified menu visits, route clicks and attributed pairing purchases than evergreen menu promotion.

## 2. Product boundary

The pilot uses only pairings that already exist in the menu data:

| Pairing | Current menu price | Pricing status |
|---|---:|---|
| Cool Lime + Macaron | 290 TRY | approved offer |
| Iced Latte + San Sebastian Cheesecake | 370 TRY | menu total |

Before publishing, the cafe owner must confirm current availability, final price, stock limits, serving presentation and any bonus cost.

The campaign must not claim that weather or mood selection is scientifically personalised. It is a lightweight menu-discovery experience.

## 3. Measurable promise

Customer promise:

> In less than 20 seconds, discover one Roby's pairing that fits your moment.

Business promise to validate:

> A narrative launch can increase paired-item orders and route intent without relying on a large discount.

## 4. Audience

Primary:

- people within practical travel distance of Gazipaşa;
- existing Instagram followers who know Roby's but order the same item;
- visitors looking for a coffee-and-dessert break;
- remote workers and laptop-friendly cafe visitors.

Secondary:

- tourists who need English or Russian guidance;
- guests choosing where to meet a friend;
- customers who normally buy only one drink.

## 5. PLF sequence

The seven days follow a simplified Product Launch Formula sequence:

1. Opportunity: introduce a better way to choose.
2. Problem: show why a long menu creates friction.
3. Ownership: let the audience identify its current mood.
4. Demonstration: reveal the mechanism and first pairing.
5. Proof: show preparation, reaction and product detail.
6. Open: activate Taste Week and the offer.
7. Close and continue: create honest urgency, then preserve the relationship.

## 6. Seven-day campaign

### Day 1 — The new opportunity

Objective: create curiosity without selling the pair yet.

Instagram Story, frame 1:

> Bugün kahve seçmek yerine anını seçsen?

Frame 2:

> Ferahlamak mı? Yavaşlamak mı? Odaklanmak mı?

Frame 3:

> Roby's Taste Week geliyor. Menüden tek bir iyi eşleşme, tam şu an için.

CTA:

> İlk öğrenen ol

Feed/Reel caption:

> Uzun bir menü yerine, bugünkü anına açılan tek bir kapı. Roby's Taste Week yakında.

Measurement:

- story reach;
- completion from frame 1 to frame 3;
- profile visits;
- replies containing a mood word.

### Day 2 — The problem

Objective: make menu indecision visible and relatable.

Story, frame 1:

> Menüye bakıp yine aynı kahveyi söylediğin oldu mu?

Frame 2 poll:

> En zor seçim hangisi?
> - İçecek
> - Tatlı

Frame 3:

> Taste Week boyunca sana tüm menüyü anlatmayacağız. Her gün yalnızca bir iyi eşleşme göstereceğiz.

CTA:

> Bugünkü ruh halini seç

Feed caption:

> Daha fazla seçenek her zaman daha iyi bir seçim demek değil. Bazen ihtiyacımız olan şey, doğru anda gelen tek bir öneridir.

Measurement:

- poll response rate;
- profile-to-site click rate;
- landing-page unique visitors.

### Day 3 — Ownership

Objective: make the visitor participate in the recommendation.

Story quiz:

1. Bugün serinlemek mi, yavaşlamak mı?
2. Hafif ve parlak mı, yoğun ve kremamsı mı?
3. Tek başına mola mı, biriyle paylaşmak mı?

Result A:

> Senin anın: Cool Lime + Makaron

Result B:

> Senin anın: Buzlu Latte + San Sebastian

CTA:

> Eşleşmemi aç

Landing CTA destination:

- result A highlights the 290 TRY pairing;
- result B highlights the 370 TRY pairing.

Measurement:

- quiz start;
- quiz completion;
- pairing selected;
- pairing card viewed.

### Day 4 — Demonstration

Objective: show how the experience works, not just the finished poster.

Reel structure:

1. Hot Gazipaşa street or work moment.
2. Three fast mood choices on the phone.
3. Pairing reveal.
4. Real drink and dessert on the table.
5. Route CTA.

Voiceover/caption:

> Önce anını seç. Sonra Roby's sana menüden tek bir eşleşme göstersin. Bugünün hafif seçimi: Cool Lime + fıstıklı makaron, 290 TRY.

Alternative second creative:

> Daha yavaş ve kremamsı bir mola: Buzlu Latte + San Sebastian, 370 TRY.

CTA:

> Bugünün eşleşmesini gör

Measurement:

- three-second views;
- 50% video completion;
- landing click-through rate;
- route-click rate after pairing view.

### Day 5 — Proof and behind the scenes

Objective: replace generic social proof with observable evidence.

Content options:

- barista preparing the drink;
- close-up of the served pair;
- one approved guest reaction;
- a short comparison: refreshing versus creamy;
- remaining stock or preparation window, only when accurate.

Story copy:

> İki farklı an. İki farklı eşleşme.

> Ferah ve canlı: Cool Lime + Makaron.

> Yavaş ve kremamsı: Buzlu Latte + San Sebastian.

Question sticker:

> Hangisini yarın denemek istersin?

Evidence rule:

Do not invent testimonials, quantities, popularity claims or limited stock. Use only owner-approved observations.

Measurement:

- replies and sticker responses;
- saves;
- shares;
- return visits to the landing page.

### Day 6 — Open Taste Week

Objective: turn accumulated intent into a cafe visit.

Hero message:

> Roby's Taste Week başladı.

Supporting copy:

> Anını seç, eşleşmeni aç ve bu hafta Roby's'de dene.

Offer stack:

1. One guided pairing recommendation.
2. The existing pairing price shown clearly.
3. A digital Taste Passport stamp after an attributed purchase.
4. A next-pairing reveal after the first tasting.
5. Optional low-cost bonus approved by the owner.

Recommended bonus order:

1. digital stamp or next reveal;
2. small cookie or simple add-on with controlled unit cost;
3. size or syrup upgrade only when operationally easy;
4. avoid percentage discount unless the owner needs a stronger acquisition lever.

CTA:

> Eşleşmemi seç

Secondary CTA:

> Yol tarifi al

Measurement:

- pairing selections;
- route clicks;
- Instagram message clicks;
- redemption-code views;
- attributed purchases.

### Day 7 — Honest close and continuation

Objective: create a deadline without fake scarcity and convert non-buyers.

Morning story:

> Taste Week'in son günü. Bugünkü anın hangi eşleşme?

Afternoon story:

> Cool Lime + Makaron mu, Buzlu Latte + San Sebastian mı?

Evening story:

> Taste Week bugün kapanıyor. Ama Taste Journey devam edecek. İlk eşleşmeni bugün aç.

CTA:

> Son gün eşleşmemi gör

After close:

- remove any expired bonus language;
- keep the evergreen Taste Journey page;
- publish the next-pairing waiting list or follow prompt;
- thank participants and report only verified outcomes.

## 7. Landing-page wireframe

### Section 1 — Hero

Headline:

> Bugünkü anına bir eşleşme.

Subhead:

> Üç kısa seçim yap. Roby's sana bu hafta deneyebileceğin tek bir içecek ve tatlı ikilisi göstersin.

Primary CTA:

> Eşleşmemi bul

Trust line:

> Kayıt gerekmez. Seçimlerin yalnızca bu cihazda tutulur.

### Section 2 — Choose the moment

Three questions:

1. Ferahlamak mı, yavaşlamak mı?
2. Hafif mi, yoğun mu?
3. Tek başına mı, paylaşarak mı?

### Section 3 — Pairing reveal

Display only one primary result, with a secondary "show another" action.

Each result contains:

- product photo;
- exact product names;
- current confirmed price;
- one-sentence reason;
- route CTA;
- save/share action;
- campaign code or QR instruction.

### Section 4 — How Taste Week works

1. Choose the moment.
2. Open one pairing.
3. Visit Roby's during the pilot.
4. Show the code or scan at the cafe.
5. Unlock the next pairing.

### Section 5 — Proof

Use owner-approved real photos, preparation details and guest reactions. Never use fabricated review text.

### Section 6 — FAQ

Required questions:

- Is this a discount?
- Are both pairings available all day?
- Can I buy the products separately?
- Does the recommendation use personal data?
- How is a Taste Passport visit confirmed?
- When does the pilot end?

### Section 7 — Final CTA

> Anını seç. Eşleşmeni aç. Roby's'de dene.

Buttons:

- Eşleşmemi bul
- Yol tarifi al

## 9. Analytics contract

Required events:

| Event | Trigger | Required properties |
|---|---|---|
| `taste_week_view` | campaign landing viewed | language, source, medium, campaign |
| `taste_week_quiz_start` | first answer selected | language, source |
| `taste_week_quiz_complete` | final answer selected | result_id, answers_count |
| `taste_week_pairing_view` | pairing result rendered | pairing_id, price_try |
| `taste_week_pairing_change` | another pairing requested | from_pairing_id, to_pairing_id |
| `taste_week_share` | share action completed | pairing_id, share_method |
| `taste_week_route_click` | route CTA clicked | pairing_id, placement |
| `taste_week_instagram_click` | Instagram CTA clicked | pairing_id, placement |
| `taste_week_code_view` | redemption code displayed | pairing_id, code_variant |
| `taste_week_purchase_confirmed` | staff confirms purchase | pairing_id, order_value_try, bonus_cost_try, code_variant |

Do not infer a purchase from a route click. Purchase confirmation requires a cafe-side action, code, receipt tag or other explicit evidence.

## 10. Attribution design

Use two visible campaign codes:

- `TASTE-A` for Cool Lime + Macaron;
- `TASTE-B` for Iced Latte + San Sebastian.

Optionally split each by source:

- `TASTE-A-IG`;
- `TASTE-A-QR`;
- `TASTE-B-IG`;
- `TASTE-B-QR`.

Staff workflow:

1. Ask whether the guest came from Taste Week.
2. Select the pairing code on a simple counter sheet or form.
3. Record order value and bonus cost.
4. Do not collect unnecessary personal data.

## 11. Economics

### Known values

- Pair A price: 290 TRY.
- Pair B price: 370 TRY.
- Equal-mix average order value: 330 TRY.

### Assumptions to validate

- gross margin: 65%;
- optional bonus unit cost: 25 TRY;
- one-time creative and setup cost: 3,000 TRY;
- traffic and conversion are scenarios, not historical facts.

### Contribution per attributed order

Without a physical bonus:

`330 × 65% = 214.50 TRY`

With a 25 TRY bonus:

`214.50 − 25 = 189.50 TRY`

### Break-even orders

Without a physical bonus:

`3,000 ÷ 214.50 = 14 attributed orders`

With a 25 TRY bonus:

`3,000 ÷ 189.50 = 16 attributed orders`

### Seven-day scenarios

| Scenario | Landing sessions | Purchase conversion | Attributed orders | Revenue | Gross contribution before setup cost | Pilot result after 3,000 TRY setup cost |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Conservative | 200 | 3% | 6 | 1,980 TRY | 1,137 TRY with bonus | -1,863 TRY |
| Base | 500 | 5% | 25 | 8,250 TRY | 4,738 TRY with bonus | +1,738 TRY |
| Strong | 1,000 | 7% | 70 | 23,100 TRY | 13,265 TRY with bonus | +10,265 TRY |

The pilot should be evaluated on incremental attributed orders, not total cafe revenue during the week.

## 12. Decision rules

### Scale

Scale or repeat when all are true:

- at least 25 attributed pairing orders;
- positive contribution after setup and bonus costs;
- landing-to-route rate at or above 8%;
- no material staff or stock disruption;
- at least 20% of attributed buyers request or unlock another pairing.

### Iterate

Iterate when:

- quiz completion is strong but route clicks are weak;
- route clicks are strong but confirmed purchases are below 15;
- one pairing receives more than 75% of selections;
- staff cannot record attribution reliably;
- the physical bonus creates operational friction.

### Stop

Stop or redesign when:

- fewer than 10 attributed orders are confirmed;
- contribution remains negative after removing one-time setup costs;
- the offer causes price confusion or customer complaints;
- campaign claims cannot be supported by current operations.

## 13. Daily dashboard

Track each day:

- story reach and completion;
- landing sessions by source;
- quiz starts and completions;
- result split between pairings;
- route clicks;
- code views;
- confirmed purchases;
- attributed revenue;
- bonus cost;
- contribution;
- stock or staff notes.

Primary formula:

`incremental contribution = attributed revenue × gross margin − bonus cost − campaign cost`

## 14. Pre-launch checklist

- [ ] Owner confirms pair availability and exact prices.
- [ ] Owner approves all public photos and copy.
- [ ] Staff receives a one-page code-recording guide.
- [ ] Campaign dates and honest close time are defined.
- [ ] Landing events are tested in all three languages.
- [ ] Route destination opens the correct Roby's location.
- [ ] Bonus stock and unit cost are known.
- [ ] No fabricated review, scarcity or popularity claim is present.
- [ ] A baseline week is recorded before launch.
- [ ] Post-pilot review is scheduled within 48 hours after close.

## 15. Recommended implementation sequence

1. Approve the offer and dates with the cafe owner.
2. Add the Taste Week landing state behind a feature flag.
3. Add the event contract and source codes.
4. Run a private staff test with at least five complete journeys.
5. Capture a seven-day pre-launch baseline.
6. Publish the seven-day sequence.
7. Close the temporary bonus exactly when promised.
8. Compare attributed contribution with the baseline and decision rules.

The strategic principle is simple:

> Launch a new way to choose, not merely a new page.
