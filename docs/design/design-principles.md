# Roby's design principles

## Product feeling

Roby's should feel like a calm premium coffee house, not a generic online catalog.

The interface should sell a feeling first:

- warm;
- calm;
- premium but not cold;
- local and human;
- easy to understand in one glance.

## Card direction

Product and pairing cards should look like cafe posters or curated recommendations.

Preferred direction:

- large product image;
- rounded poster frame;
- soft shadow;
- restrained gold or ruby accents;
- minimal text;
- price visible without hunting;
- one clear action or destination.

Avoid:

- crowded catalog blocks;
- too many badges;
- small product photos;
- inconsistent crop or color temperature;
- mixing too many visual styles on one page.

## Pairing cards

Pairings are not ordinary products. They should feel selected by the cafe.

Good labels:

- Pair of the Day;
- Today's Pairing;
- Barista Pick;
- Guest Favorite;
- Perfect Match.

The first pairing can be stronger than the rest, but the system should still be consistent.

## Mobile-first rules

Most users will judge the design on mobile.

Required:

- no horizontal overflow;
- clear tap targets;
- readable price;
- image crop must show the product, not only background;
- main CTA or next step must be obvious;
- language switch must remain usable.

## Copy rules

Use simple words. Do not make the user decode marketing poetry.

Preferred:

```text
Cool Lime + Macaron
Fresh lime. Pistachio macaron. Perfect match.
```

Avoid unclear labels when a simpler phrase sells better.

## Human taste gate

Some decisions cannot be fully automated. Human review is required when a PR changes:

- hero section feeling;
- main visual hierarchy;
- product photography style;
- card style;
- brand tone;
- color system;
- prices or offer perception.
